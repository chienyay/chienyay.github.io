Monsta X (Korean: 몬스타엑스; stylized as MONSTA X) is a South Korean boy group formed through the reality survival program No.Mercy by Starship Entertainment. The group is currently composed of six members: Shownu, Minhyuk, Kihyun, Hyungwon, Joohoney and I.M, with former member Wonho leaving the group in October 2019.

The group debuted on May 14, 2015 with their first EP Trespass.[5] In March 2017, Monsta X released their first studio album and final part of The Clan series The Clan Pt. 2.5: The Final Chapter.[6] Since 2018, Monsta X had four Japanese singles certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of Japan. Since 2019, six of their Korean albums had been certified platinum by the Korea Music Content Association. In February 2020, Monsta X's first English album All About Luv ranked number five on the US Billboard 200 and number seven on the US Rolling Stone Top 200.[7][8]

Since debut, Monsta X has been known for their aggressive style, combining elements of hip hop, EDM and pop.[1][9] This aggressive style with "tracks centred around loud, clattering, electronic instrumentals, vicious rap verses and striking vocals" has been influential across the fourth generation of K-pop.[10]

In January, the group was announced as global brand ambadassors for the cosmetics brand Urban Decay.[117][118] The group was also announced to be one of the artists featuring on NCSoft's new platform Universe, beginning with an original series called Area 51: The Code.[119]

Monsta X also released a new Japanese single album on March 10, with the lead single "Wanted" and another original Japanese track "Neo Universe".[120] The lead single "Wanted" was pre-released ahead of the physical album release on February 10, and the music video was released on February 13.[121][122] Following the release of "Wanted", Monsta X announced the release of their third Japanese studio album Flavors of Love, released on May 5, which includes their Japanese singles released since 2020, as well as five new Japanese tracks.[123]

In May, the group announced the release of their ninth EP One of a Kind and its lead single "Gambler", set for June 1.[124] Starship announced that Shownu will take a hiatus from the group and will not participate in the group's upcoming promotional activities due to resurfacing issues of retinal detachment.[125]

In October, Monsta X was announced to be joining iHeartRadio's Jingle Ball Tour. The group is set to perform at Philadelphia on December 13, Washington D.C. on December 14, Atlanta on December 16, and Miami on December 19.[130][131] Monsta X also announced their second English-language album The Dreaming, to be released on December 10, which includes the previously released single "One Day" and nine new tracks,[132] as well as releasing a documentary/concert film Monsta X: The Dreaming on December 8 in South Korea and on December 9 and 11 in seventy countries around the world. The film will contain exclusive interviews with the members, clips from their US activities, special stages, and the group's six-year journey.[133][134]

On November 19, the group released their tenth EP No Limit with the lead single "Rush Hour".[135][136] It was the group's first release to not feature Shownu since his enlistment.[137]
